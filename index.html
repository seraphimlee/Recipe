Kimchi Fried Rice Recipe

An easy & delicious classic Korean dish made with simple ingredients and a short cooking time!

Traditional Korean Dish - Kimchi Fried Rice

Typically serves 2 people

Cooking time: 15-20 minutes

It's great for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, you can have this traditional goodie anytime!

While meat can be an optional addition, this is also a great vegetarian choice when looking for a quick meal. Comprised of mainly

chili paste and kimchi, this spicy rice bowl is both filling and packs quite a punch on the taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 3 cups of rice
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 a stick of butter (can replace with cooking oil)
  • 1 sheet of dried seaweed
  • 1/4 cup of kimchi juice
  • 1 cup of kimchi, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 2-3 tbsps of Korean Red Chile Paste also known as Gochujang
  • Other additions like extra vegetables (carrots, potatoes), meat additions (spam, bacon), or sesame seeds are optional

How to Make

  1. Heat up a pan and add the butter till fully melted.
  2. Add the kimchi, minced garlic, and yellow onion and stir fry till the onions are clear.
  3. Add the rice, kimchi juice, water and gochujang and stir the ingredients together for about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the sesame oil and turn off the heat. Completely stir till the oil is evenly distributed.
  5. In a separate pan, cook the 2 eggs sunny-side up style. Add them on top of the rice.
  6. Cut up the dried seaweed into small pieces and sprinkle them along with the chopped green onions on top, and you're ready to eat!
